# NourishBridge

## Connecting Food. Nourishing Lives.

### Smart Food Redistribution Platform.

### Description.

NourishBridge is a Smart Food Redistribution Platform designed to reduce food waste by connecting food donors with verified NGOs, orphanages, old-age homes, and other charitable organizations.

The platform enables individuals, event organizers, restaurants, and businesses to donate surplus food through a secure and transparent system. Using location-based services, NourishBridge helps donors find nearby organizations that can receive food quickly and efficiently.

The platform also promotes transparency through donation tracking, verified NGO profiles, and administrative monitoring, ensuring that donated food reaches people who need it most.


### Problem Statement

Food waste has become a significant challenge, particularly during weddings, parties, corporate events, restaurants, and other large gatherings where food is prepared in bulk. A considerable portion of surplus food often remains unused and is discarded despite being safe for consumption. At the same time, many orphanages, old-age homes, NGOs, destitute shelters, and underprivileged communities continue to face food shortages.

The primary issue is not merely the availability of surplus food but the absence of a reliable and organized platform that connects food donors with charitable organizations in a timely manner. Existing donation methods often rely on personal contacts or manual communication, making the process slow, inefficient, and difficult to coordinate. This frequently results in unnecessary food wastage and missed opportunities to support people in need.

NourishBridge addresses this problem by providing a secure and technology-driven Smart Food Redistribution Platform that enables donors to locate nearby verified NGOs, donate surplus food efficiently, track the donation process, and ensure that food reaches the intended beneficiaries in a transparent and trustworthy manner.

### Existing System

At present, surplus food generated during weddings, parties, restaurants, corporate events, and other large gatherings is primarily managed through informal and manual methods. Food donors typically rely on personal contacts, phone calls, social media groups, or local NGO networks to identify organizations willing to accept food donations. In many cases, if a suitable recipient cannot be found within a short period, the surplus food is discarded despite being safe for consumption.

The current approach lacks a centralized digital platform that connects food donors with verified NGOs, orphanages, old-age homes, and charitable organizations. As a result, donors often face difficulties in locating nearby organizations, coordinating food pickups, and confirming whether the donated food has reached its intended beneficiaries.

Additionally, existing methods provide limited transparency, no real-time donation tracking, and no standardized verification process for participating organizations. These limitations reduce the efficiency of food redistribution and contribute to unnecessary food wastage while many communities continue to experience food insecurity.

#### Limitations of Existing System

- Relies on manual communication and personal contacts.
- No centralized food donation platform.
- Difficulty locating nearby verified NGOs.
- No real-time donation tracking.
- Lack of transparency in the donation process.
- Food often gets wasted due to delays.
- No proper donation history or reporting.
- Limited trust between donors and organizations.


### Proposed System

NourishBridge is a Smart Food Redistribution Platform designed to efficiently connect surplus food donors with verified NGOs, orphanages, old-age homes, destitute shelters, and other charitable organizations through a centralized digital platform. The system aims to reduce food waste while ensuring that safe and edible surplus food reaches people in need quickly and transparently.

The proposed system enables individuals, event organizers, restaurants, hotels, and businesses to register and submit food donation requests by providing details such as food type, quantity, pickup location, preparation time, and food images. Using location-based services, the platform identifies nearby verified organizations that are eligible to receive the donation, reducing delays in food collection.

NourishBridge also provides secure user authentication, role-based access for donors, NGOs, volunteers, and administrators, and a donation tracking mechanism that allows users to monitor the status of each donation from submission to successful delivery. An administrative dashboard is included to verify NGOs, manage users, monitor donation activities, and maintain transparency across the platform.

By replacing manual communication with an organized, technology-driven solution, NourishBridge improves the efficiency, reliability, and trustworthiness of the food donation process while contributing to the reduction of food waste and supporting communities facing food insecurity.

### Target Users

NourishBridge is designed to serve multiple categories of users involved in the food donation ecosystem.

1. Food Donors

Individuals, event organizers, restaurants, hotels, catering services, companies, and institutions that wish to donate surplus food safely and efficiently.

2. NGOs and Charitable Organizations

Verified non-governmental organizations, orphanages, old-age homes, destitute shelters, community kitchens, and food banks that receive donated food for distribution to beneficiaries.

3. Volunteers

Registered volunteers responsible for coordinating food collection and delivery whenever required.

4. System Administrator

The administrator manages the platform by verifying NGOs, monitoring donation activities, managing users, resolving issues, and ensuring transparency and security.

5. Beneficiaries (Indirect Users)

People who ultimately receive the donated food, including children, senior citizens, homeless individuals, and economically disadvantaged communities.
